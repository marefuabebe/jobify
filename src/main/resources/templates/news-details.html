Jobify Launches New AI-Powered Matching Algorithm

A standardized leap forward in how we connect talent with opportunity.

SHARE

San Francisco, CA — Today, Jobify unveiled its most significant platform update to date: a proprietary AI-powered matching engine designed to reduce hiring time by 50% while improving match quality.

The new system, dubbed "Jobify Neural Match," moves beyond simple keyword matching to understand the semantic context of job descriptions and freelancer portfolios. By analyzing successful past contracts, skill assessments, and behavioral data, the algorithm can predict compatibility with unprecedented accuracy.

Beyond Keywords

"Traditional search is broken," said Jane Doe, VP of Product at Jobify. "It relies on users knowing exactly what keywords to search for. Our new AI understands intent. If a client asks for a 'React developer who knows design,' our system knows to look for candidates with strong frontend skills and UI/UX portfolio pieces, even if they don't explicitly list those exact keywords in that order."

"This isn't just about speed. It's about finding the *right* person. In our beta testing, client satisfaction scores increased by 30%."

Key Features

  • Semantic Search: Understands natural language queries like "Find me a logo designer with a minimalist style."
  • Skill Verification: Automatically cross-references claimed skills with portfolio evidence and past project ratings.
  • Bias Reduction: The algorithm is trained to prioritize skills and experience, actively mitigating unconscious bias in the hiring process.

Availability

The new matching engine is live today for all Enterprise clients and will be rolling out to all users over the next two weeks. We’re excited to see how this technology empowers our community to do their best work.
